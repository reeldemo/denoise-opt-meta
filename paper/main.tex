\begin{abstract}
Open wrap seams in periodic wavetable cycles inject audible \emph{crackle} under
cyclic playback. We cast bake-time periodization as an unsupervised optimization
problem: a seam-local stack $f_\theta$ with $\theta\in[0,1]^{12}$ is scored by a
joint denoise/shape loss without paired clean labels. After constructing a
procedural 100\,000-cycle bench (FFT-style extreme overlays, mix modes, forced
cliffs), we run a \textbf{1500-trial} literature-informed meta-learning /
hyperparameter search whose priors follow Bayesian HPO localization, population-based
training (PBT) mutation, multi-objective shape preference, evolutionary explore,
and algorithm-configuration racing. On held-out validation ($N{=}2000$), a
\textbf{five-algorithm matrix} compares a naive DualCosine baseline against the
top-4 meta champions. Meta Top~1 reaches $Q\approx 0.790$ vs naive
$Q\approx 0.789$ while keeping $\mathcal{S}\approx 0.997$. We survey related
work in VA anti-aliasing, wavetable / DDSP, label-free restoration, and AutoML,
explicitly listing which sources informed our priors and which were screened out.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Wavetable synthesizers store one-period frames and wrap phase modulo one.
Discontinuous ends $r[0]\neq r[N-1]$ produce periodic broadband clicks.
Classical DualCosine / Soft fades and live BLEP address related phenomena, but
edited Quant frames need a fast bake operator that is both aggressive on wrap
energy and conservative on mid-cycle timbre.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
(1)~Measurable functionals $\mathcal{D},\mathcal{S}$ and loss
$L=(1-\mathcal{D})+\lambda(1-\mathcal{S})$.
(2)~DenoiseOpt seam-local inference with exact mid-cycle copy.
(3)~100k procedural dataset for unlabeled fit.
(4)~1500 lit-informed meta trials with six prior families.
(5)~Five-algorithm benchmark matrix (naive + top-4).
(6)~Open synth and meta-learning repositories.

\section{Related work and literature triage}
Literature was harvested with the Klaut Research MCP (local OpenAlex / Crossref /
arXiv providers; hosted \texttt{research.klaut.pro} was misrouted during this study).
We distinguish \emph{used} vs \emph{screened} sources.

\subsection{Used --- virtual analog \& wavetable DSP}
V\"alim\"aki \& Huovilainen's survey of VA oscillator/filter algorithms and
aliasing control~\cite{valimaki2006va} and subsequent BLEP/BLAMP / antialiasing
work~\cite{esqueda2016aliasing,esqueda2016blamp,valimaki2007aa} motivate treating
discontinuities as first-class DSP objects. Differentiable wavetable / DDSP
reviews~\cite{shan2021dwt,ddsp_review2024} show learnable tables but target
different objectives than bake wrap close.

\subsection{Used --- label-free restoration}
Noise2Noise~\cite{lehtinen2018n2n} shows restoration without clean targets when
noise statistics are structured. We adopt the same philosophy at cycle scale:
the ``noise'' is wrap cliff energy measured by $C(\cdot)$.

\subsection{Used --- meta-learning \& hyperparameter optimization}
Practical Bayesian optimization of ML algorithms~\cite{snoek2012bo} and later HPO
surveys~\cite{bischl2023hpo} motivate densifying search near previously good
$\lambda$ (\texttt{bayes\_local} prior). Population-based training ideas
(exploit/mutate elite configs) inform \texttt{pbt\_exploit}. Algorithm
configuration / iterated racing~\cite{lopez2016irace} inform \texttt{racing\_mid}.
Multi-objective evolutionary decomposition~\cite{zhang2007moead} informs
\texttt{mo\_shape} (higher $\lambda$). Wide evolutionary explore covers
\texttt{evo\_explore}.

\subsection{Screened / not used as design drivers}
High-citation but off-domain hits (medical imaging surveys, federated learning,
prompting surveys, molecular ML) appeared in broad keyword harvests and were
\textbf{not} used to set priors. Generic optimizers (Adam~\cite{kingma2015adam})
are assumed as ambient ML context but do not define our outer loop. MAML-style
few-shot gradient meta-learning~\cite{finn2017maml} is related philosophically
yet not applicable: we have no task-gradient through a deep net, only a
12-parameter bake stack.

\section{Problem formulation}
\begin{align}
C(x) &= 2\,\mathrm{wrap}(x)+\mathrm{max\_step}(x)+0.35\,\mathrm{hf}_{\mathrm{proxy}}(x),\\
\mathcal{D} &= \mathrm{clamp}\!\big((C(r)-C(y))/\max(C(r),\varepsilon),0,1\big),\\
\mathcal{S} &= 1-\mathrm{clamp}\!\big(\mathrm{MAE}_{\mathrm{mid}}(y,r)/(\mathrm{RMS}(r)+\varepsilon),0,1\big),\\
L &= (1-\mathcal{D})+\lambda(1-\mathcal{S}),\qquad Q=\tfrac12(\mathcal{D}+\mathcal{S}).
\end{align}
Shape invariant: $y[i]=r[i]$ outside fade zones.

\subsection{Dataset}
Seeds $0..10^5{-}1$ generate ten families (morph, harmonic\_fft, extreme\_overlay,
combo modes, quant\_steps, nonlinear, am\_fm, noise\_tone, triple\_mix,
open\_wrap\_bias) at $N{=}256$.

\subsection{1500-trial meta-learning}
Each trial samples a prior family, mutates $\theta$ near the frozen champion,
applies fade/polish/pin/detrend biases, and scores on 400 held-out cycles.
Top-12 are revalidated on 2000 cycles with family stress tests.

\section{Discussion}
Gains over DualCosine are small but consistent under a hard shape floor
($\mathcal{S}\ge 0.97$). The method's value is a \emph{principled, reproducible}
selection of $\theta^\star$ and an auditable link from AutoML literature to DSP
knobs. Residual crackle in combo/nonlinear families remains a live-BLEP concern.

\section{Limitations}
Bake metrics $\neq$ perceptual MOS; no MAML-style task gradients; hosted Klaut
gateway routing was unavailable (local MCP used). Meta search mutates around a
strong frozen init---a cold-start evolutionary run may explore further.

\section{Conclusion}
DenoiseOpt combines label-free seam optimization, a 100k procedural bench, and
1500 literature-informed meta trials. Frozen $\theta^\star$ ships in ReelSynth
(\texttt{Seam·Opt}); artifacts live in the public meta-learning repository.
