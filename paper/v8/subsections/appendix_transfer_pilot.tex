\section{Transfer pilot on CWRU bearings and MIT-BIH ECG}
\label{app:transfer-pilot}

We briefly transfer the winning outer loop (\textbf{Ours / hybrid GA--PPO}, approach code \texttt{hybrid\_lstm})
to public cycle-local wrap tasks outside musical wavetables:
Case Western Reserve University (CWRU) bearing acceleration periods and MIT-BIH ECG beat windows
($L{=}256$; prolonged residual $R$ vs an ideal sibling constructed by domain-specific periodization).
Table~\ref{tab:transfer-pilot} summarizes holdout-refit $R$ against a \emph{classical} board
(no-bake, DualCosine, fades, endpoint pin, seam FIR, and domain classical proxies).
Artifacts: \texttt{reelsynth/brand/artifacts/signal\_heal\_transfer/};
prose source: \texttt{denoise-opt-meta/docs/SIGNAL\_HEAL\_TRANSFER\_PILOT.md}.

\paragraph{Classical-board disclaimer (required).}
This appendix compares against classical / domain-proxy baselines only.
It is \emph{not} a claim of beating published deep learning SOTA on CWRU diagnosis or ECG enhancement
(e.g., Cycle-GAN, BeatDiff, or clinical denoisers) unless those models were executed under the same residual protocol---they were not.
Outer-loop budget is modest ($250$ outer iters / dataset, \texttt{fit\_steps}${}={}40$): a transfer pilot, not a multi-day domain campaign.
Real content is z-scored per period; absolute clinical/engineering scale is not preserved.
